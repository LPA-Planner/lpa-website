Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about planning enforcement or how LPA Planner works? Here are clear answers to the most common questions people ask before starting a service.

What is planning enforcement?

Planning enforcement is one of the three “pillars” of the planning system; along with "law and policies" and "applications and appeals".
It deals with breaches of planning control, such as unauthorised building works or changes of use. Enforcement addresses the harm caused by planning breaches, working for the benefit of both the developers (who may not realise the nuances of the planning system) and communities (who may be adversely impacted by planning breaches).

What is an LPA?

LPA stands for Local Planning Authority. This is usually your local council, responsible for deciding planning applications and investigating breaches. LPA Planner reflects over a decade of experience working inside LPAs, and now offers independent advice to help homeowners, agents, and businesses navigate the system with clarity.

Why choose LPA Planner?

Unlike most planning agents, LPA Planner specialises in enforcement matters. The services and fees are clear, with upfront non-committal scope of works, and you’ll always get a written outcome (options letter, report, or document suite) with practical next steps. By comparison, many agents only focus on applications, with little transparency on fees or turnaround. LPA Planner fills that gap.

  • Planning agents seeking specialist enforcement support.
  • Small businesses wanting clarity on premises or compliance.

Our fixed-fee, timebound services make professional planning advice accessible without the cost of a full consultancy.

How quickly will I get a response?

LPA Planner reviews all submissions from the enquiry form, which are responded to within 2 working days. This is either an email rejection or a response with a scope of works and an invoice. Work begins once requested documents and payments have been submitted and cleared.
The response letter for the Quick Review service is issued within 2 working days after work begins.
The report for the Document Review service is issued within 5 working days after works begins.
The responses to the Appeal Submission service are aligned with statutory deadlines.
Urgent responses may be available for an additional fee.

Do you provide in-person services?

No. LPA Planner provides remote-only services, with advice delivered by email. Site specifics can usually be understood from documents, maps, and photos. Video calls can be arranged where needed.

What happens if I don’t pay, or I change my mind?

If you don’t pay, that will be treated as a cancellation of services, as explained in our Terms. If you pay, and decide to cancel before receiving the documented outcome (letter, report, etc.) from your chosen service, you will be refunded in line with the Terms.
