Planning Enforcement: Why LPA Planner?

Planning enforcement deals with issues such as unauthorised building works or changes of use. The process can feel complex and intimidating, with strict deadlines, technical terminology, and sometimes conflicting advice.

With over a decade of experience working in Local Planning Authorities, LPA Planner brings that expertise directly to homeowners, architects, and developers, with years of experience:

  • Drafting, serving, and defending formal planning notices including Enforcement Notices, Breach of Condition Notices, and Planning Contravention Notices.
  • Leading successful prosecutions for planning offences, acting as a professional witness and giving evidence in court.
  • Assessing planning applications ranging from householder extensions and Lawful Development Certificates to complex commercial schemes and major developments.
  • Representing councils at planning committees and public inquiries.
  • Advising planning officers, councillors, and legal teams on enforcement strategy and decision-making.

LPA Planner was created to make planning advice clear, practical, and accessible, even for complex matters or enforcement investigations. Every service has clear, upfront fees and defined timeframes, so you know what to expect before work begins.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about planning enforcement or how LPA Planner works? Here are clear answers to the most common questions people ask before starting a service.

What is planning enforcement?

Planning enforcement is one of the three “pillars” of the planning system — alongside making applications and developing policy. It deals with breaches of planning control, such as unauthorised building works or changes of use. Enforcement ensures that development is fair and balanced, protecting both communities and the planning system.

What is an LPA?

LPA stands for Local Planning Authority. This is usually your local council, responsible for deciding planning applications and investigating breaches. LPA Planner reflects over a decade of experience working inside LPAs, and now offers independent advice to help homeowners, agents, and businesses navigate the system with clarity.

Why choose LPA Planner?

Unlike most planning agents, LPA Planner specialises in enforcement matters. The services are clear, with upfront fees and non-committal, and you’ll always get a written outcome (options letter, report, or document suite) with practical next steps. By comparison, many agents only focus on applications, with little transparency on fees or turnaround. LPA Planner fills that gap.

How quickly will I get a response?

Responses are provided once documents and payments have been made. Quick Reviews are issued within 2 working days, Document Reviews within 5 working days, and Appeals are aligned with statutory deadlines. Urgent same-day responses may be available for an additional fee.

Do you provide in-person services?

No. LPA Planner provides remote-only services, with advice delivered by email. Site specifics can usually be understood from documents, maps, and photos. Video calls can be arranged where needed.

What happens if I don’t pay, or I change my mind?

If you don’t pay, that will be treated as a cancellation of services, as explained in our Terms. If you pay, and decide to cancel before receiving the documented outcome from your chosen service, you will be refunded in line with the Terms.
